Introduction

Plastics are polymer materials made from repeating monomers. This website compares bioplastics (derived from renewable biological sources) and petroleum-based plastics (derived from fossil fuels). The goal is to understand their chemistry, environmental impact, production process, and sustainability.

Bioplastics

Bioplastics are polymers made from renewable resources such as corn starch, sugarcane, or cellulose. Some are biodegradable, while others are simply bio-based.

PLA (Polylactic Acid)

Made from fermented plant starch. Often used in packaging and 3D printing.

Biodegradable Bags

Designed to break down under composting conditions.

Starch-Based Plastics

Made by modifying natural starch polymers.

Bio Packaging

Eco-friendly packaging made from renewable biomass.

Petroleum-Based Plastics

Petroleum plastics are synthetic polymers derived from crude oil or natural gas. They are durable, inexpensive, and widely used.

Polyethylene (PE)

Used in plastic bags and containers.

Polyethylene Terephthalate (PET)

Commonly used in water bottles and packaging.

Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC)

Used in pipes, cables, and construction materials.

Polystyrene (PS)

Used in disposable cups and packaging foam.
